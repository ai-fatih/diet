# Whole-genome methylation profiling of menstrual stem cells identifies novel biomarkers for endometriosis

**Published:** bioRxiv (preprint), July 2025  
**DOI:** 10.1101/2025.07.25.666720

- Clinical study: n=42 (19 endometriosis, 23 controls).
- Method: Enzymatic Methylation Sequencing (EM-seq) on FACS-isolated MenSCs from menstrual blood.
- Key finding: Differentially Methylated Regions (DMRs) enriched in genes linked to endometriosis hallmarks — inflammation, tissue remodeling, development.
- Machine learning models: Specificity 83%, Sensitivity 79%.
- DMRs map to WNT signaling, stem cell regulation, extracellular matrix remodeling.
- Integration with scRNA-seq (HECA dataset) showed methylation changes correlate with transcriptional suppression in MenSC-like stromal populations.
- Freshly isolated MenSCs avoid the culturing bias that alters cell properties.
- Menstrual blood collection standardizes sample timing across participants (avoids cycle phase variability).
- EM-seq avoids harsh bisulfite treatment, better preserves DNA integrity.
- Study overcomes limitations of previous array-based methods (which cover only 1.5-3% of CpG sites).
- eMSCs comprise only 0.02% to 1.23% of endometrial cells — need cell-type specific analysis.
- Genes identified: ZNF516, TPGS1, GPM6B, CLMP, PPFIA1 — downregulated transcriptionally.
- Conclusion: MenSC DNA methylation profiling is a promising non-invasive approach for early diagnosis.
