# Comparative Analysis of Endometrial, Vaginal, and Gut Microbiota in Patients with and without Adenomyosis

**Journal:** Acta Obstetricia et Gynecologica Scandinavica 2024;103:1271–1282  
**DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1111/aogs.14847  
**Authors:** Valdés-Bango M, Gracia M, Rubio E, Vergara A, Casals-Pascual C, Ros C, Rius M, Martínez-Zamora MÁ, Mension E, Quintas L, et al.

---

## Original (English)

### Background
Adenomyosis is a benign gynecological condition characterized by the presence of endometrial glands and stroma within the myometrium. Its pathogenesis remains poorly understood. This study aimed to characterize the microbiota of the endometrium, vagina, and gut in women with and without adenomyosis.

### Methods
- **Design:** Prospective cohort
- **Population:** 38 women with adenomyosis vs. 46 healthy controls
- **Samples:** Fecal, endometrial, and vaginal
- **Analysis:** 16S rRNA sequencing (Illumina platform, V3–V4 region)

### Key Findings
#### Gut microbiota (fecal)
- ↑ Enriched: **Rhodospirillales** (order), **Ruminococcus gauvreauii group** (genus), **Ruminococcaceae**, **Actinomyces**
- ↓ Depleted: several protective taxa
- Alpha diversity (Chao index): Control 181.87 ± 45.50 vs. Adenomyosis 138.27 ± 66.63 (not statistically significant)
- Shannon: Control 3.22 ± 0.58 vs. Adenomyosis 2.93 ± 0.81
- Beta diversity (Bray–Curtis): significant separation between groups

#### Endometrial microbiota
- Similar enrichment patterns observed as in gut (Rhodospirillales, Ruminococcus gauvreauii group)
- Overlap between gut and endometrial dysbiosis suggests a systemic rather than site-specific alteration

#### Vaginal microbiota
- Less pronounced differences compared to gut and endometrium

### Conclusions
Women with adenomyosis exhibit **overlapping microbial alterations in gut and endometrium**, characterized by enrichment of specific taxa (Rhodospirillales, R. gauvreauii) and depletion of protective commensals. This is the **first study to specifically examine the microbiome in adenomyosis** across multiple body sites.

## Резюме на русском

### Микробиом при аденомиозе: первое исследование (Valdés-Bango 2024)

**Единственное исследование**, отдельно изучавшее микробиом **именно при аденомиозе** (не эндометриозе в целом).

**Дизайн:** 38 женщин с аденомиозом vs 46 здоровых. Анализ фекалий, эндометрия и влагалища (16S rRNA).

**Главные находки в кишечнике:**
- ↑ *Rhodospirillales* (порядок)
- ↑ *Ruminococcus gauvreauii* group
- ↑ *Ruminococcaceae*, *Actinomyces*
- ↓ Защитные таксоны

**Ключевой вывод:** Дисбиоз обнаружен **и в кишечнике, и в эндометрии** — паттерн схож, что указывает на **системный**, а не локальный характер изменений. Это подтверждает гипотезу о роли оси "кишечник-матка" в патогенезе аденомиоза.

**Значение для вашего случая:** Учитывая подтверждённый аденомиоз (УЗИ 24.05.2026) и общую MC-ось, эти данные directly supporting the unified microbiome-MC-inflammation model.
