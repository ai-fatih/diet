## 1. ACOG 2026 Diagnostic Guidelines (March 2026)

The American College of Obstetricians and Gynecologists published its **first-ever comprehensive guidelines** for diagnosing endometriosis.

- **Clinical diagnosis is now sufficient** — no surgery required to begin treatment
- Doctors should suspect endometriosis when a patient has ≥1 of: chronic pelvic pain, dysmenorrhea, dyspareunia, painful urination, painful bowel movements, or infertility
- Transvaginal ultrasound is the recommended first imaging step
- Blood/urine biomarkers are **not yet accurate enough** for diagnosis
- Adolescents are now explicitly included (endo can start early)
- Surgery is no longer the mandatory first step; treatment decisions are shared patient-doctor

*Source: Rescripted / ACOG*

## 2. Non-Invasive Diagnosis via Menstrual Blood (Endogene.bio)

A study in *Nature Communications Medicine* (May 16, 2026) demonstrated a breakthrough:

- **DNA methylation patterns** in menstrual blood-derived stem cells (MenSCs) distinguish endometriosis patients from healthy women
- 81% accuracy — clearly differentiated the two groups
- 400+ genomic regions with differential methylation, linked to inflammation and abnormal growth genes
- Machine learning models trained on methylation signatures can predict disease status
- Non-invasive — uses menstrual blood collected at home
- Could reduce diagnosis from 7-10 years to weeks

*Sources: ThePrint, National Geographic, OGP News*

## 3. ENDO-205 — First Disease-Modifying Therapy (EndoCyclic Therapeutics)

- **Precision peptide therapy** — engineered for selective uptake into endometriotic cells
- Triggers **apoptosis** (programmed cell death) of lesions
- Demonstrated **complete elimination** of endometriosis lesions and inflammation in preclinical studies
- FDA **IND clearance** in March 2026 — moving to first-in-human trials
- Company also developing **FemLUNA** — a non-radioactive imaging agent to visualize lesions without surgery
- Described as potentially "the next GLP-1-scale opportunity in women's health"

*Source: Drug Discovery News*

## 4. SPECT-CT Nuclear Imaging for "Invisible" Endometriosis

- Phase 2 study in *The Lancet* (May 2026) using **⁹⁹mTc-maraciclatide SPECT-CT**
- Targets **angiogenesis** (blood vessel formation) in endometriosis lesions
- ~84% concordance with surgical findings
- Can detect **superficial peritoneal disease** — the most common subtype, usually invisible on ultrasound/MRI
- Could reduce need for diagnostic laparoscopy

*Source: The Lancet / Fertility News*

## 5. Neurofilament Light Chain (NfL) — Nerve Injury Biomarker

- Study in *Pain Reports* — serum NfL (marker of neuronal injury) elevated in endometriosis patients vs healthy controls
- Supports emerging view of endometriosis as **neuro-inflammatory disease**, not just a gynecological one
- 26% of endometriosis patients met neuropathic-like pain criteria
- Highlights pain heterogeneity — neuronal injury and pain perception may not fully overlap

*Source: EndoNews*

## 6. Bacterial Hypothesis — Fusobacterium & Endometriosis

- Growing evidence that **Fusobacterium** in the endometrium may contribute to lesion formation
- Bacteria → macrophage activation (M2 state) → TGF-β → transformation of fibroblasts into myofibroblasts → better adhesion and proliferation at ectopic sites
- 64% of endometriosis patients positive for *Fusobacterium* in endometrium
- Antibiotics reduced lesions in mouse models
- Non-hormonal therapeutic avenue still under investigation

*Source: iScience Review, Fierce by mitú*

## 7. GLP-1 Agonists (Ozempic, Mounjaro) for Endometriosis Symptoms

- Growing off-label use and patient reports of symptom improvement
- Anti-inflammatory effects via TNF-alpha, IL-6, CRP reduction
- Improved insulin sensitivity may reduce estrogen-driven inflammation
- 68% of surveyed women reported significant reduction in ≥1 symptom on GLP-1 medications
- **No large clinical trials yet** for endometriosis specifically
- Not a cure — but increasingly used as part of symptom management

*Source: EndoMe, FastFit Podcast*

## 8. Largest Genetic Study — 80 New Genomic Regions (Nature Genetics, April 2026)

- Analyzed 1.4 million women (105,000+ with endometriosis)
- Identified **80 genomic regions** associated with endometriosis risk (37 previously unknown)
- Genetic links to migraine, anxiety, nausea
- Shared genetic regions between endometriosis and adenomyosis
- Paves way for personalized treatment approaches

*Source: O Globo / Nature Genetics*

## 9. Increased Risk of Premature Ovarian Insufficiency & Surgical Menopause

- Endometriosis patients have **3.7× risk** of primary ovarian insufficiency and **12.4× risk** of surgical menopause
- Rates of oophorectomy and hysterectomy rising over the past decade
- Emphasizes need for early diagnosis and fertility counseling

*Source: EM-Consulte / Elsevier*

- £1 million for menstrual health education in schools
- Redesigned clinical pathways for pelvic pain and heavy bleeding
- Community-based care for endometriosis moving away from hospital-only model
- Focus on reducing diagnostic delays and improving informed consent

*Source: UK Government / DHSC*

## Резюме на русском языке

В мае-июне 2026 года произошёл ряд прорывов в диагностике и лечении эндометриоза:

1. **Новые диагностические guidelines ACOG** (март 2026) — теперь можно ставить клинический диагноз на основе симптомов, без хирургии.
2. **Диагностика по менструальной крови** — анализ метилирования ДНК в стволовых клетках менструальной крови позволяет выявить эндометриоз с точностью 81% (Nature Communications Medicine).
3. **ENDO-205** — первый препарат, модифицирующий течение болезни: пептид, избирательно уничтожающий очаги эндометриоза через апоптоз (FDA одобрило临床试验).
4. **СПЕКТ-КТ визуализация** — новый метод ядерной визуализации выявляет «невидимый» поверхностный эндометриоз (84% совпадение с хирургией).
5. **Биомаркер повреждения нервов** — нейрофиламент легкой цепи (NfL) в крови повышен при эндометриозе, подтверждая нейровоспалительную природу болезни.
6. **Бактериальная теория** — *Fusobacterium* может способствовать образованию очагов; антибиотики уменьшают поражения на животных моделях.
7. **Giant genetic study** — 80 геномных регионов, связанных с риском эндометриоза (Nature Genetics).

Главный вывод: эндометриоз перестаёт считаться чисто гинекологическим заболеванием — его всё чаще рассматривают как системное нейровоспалительное заболевание, а диагностика смещается от инвазивной хирургии к неинвазивным методам (менструальная кровь, визуализация, биомаркеры).
