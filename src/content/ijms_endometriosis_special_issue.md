## Description

Endometriosis is a polymorphous and subtle pathology that affects 1 in 10 women worldwide. Endometriosis-related pain severely affects people's quality of life by impairing working efficiency, social life and sexual function, among others. According to the type and location of the lesions, endometriosis is subdivided into peritoneal endometriosis, deep endometriosis and endometrioma. Although research has improved our knowledge of the genetic and molecular pathways involved in the establishment and progression of endometriosis, the pathogenesis of this condition remains yet to be fully understood.

This Special Issue of IJMS highlights recent insights and discoveries, novel developments, current challenges, and future perspectives in the field of endometriosis.

## Published Papers (10 papers)

### 1. Dyspareunia and Biomarkers: A Case Study of Sexual Dysfunction in Moderate Endometriosis
- **Authors:** Nati et al.
- **Published:** 27 Dec 2024
- **Key finding:** Elevated IL-8 tissue levels negatively correlated with desire (r = −0.649) and satisfaction (r = −0.813); higher BDNF associated with increased pain. Physical activity improved pain scores.

### 2. Eutopic and Ectopic Endometrial IL-17 and IL-17R Expression in Adenomyosis
- **Authors:** Hsu et al.
- **Published:** 17 Oct 2024
- **Key finding:** IL-17A and IL-17R significantly higher in adenomyosis vs eutopic endometrium (7.28-fold and 1.99-fold, respectively), suggesting IL-17 system involvement.

### 3. Regulation of MicroRNA-21 by IL-6 in Fibrosis Development in Endometriotic Lesions
- **Authors:** Ochoa Bernal et al.
- **Published:** 19 Aug 2024
- **Key finding:** miR-21 regulated by IL-6 contributes to fibrosis in endometriosis via TGF-β signaling. Inflammation and fibrosis present at very early stage of endometriosis. Proposes redefinition of endometriosis as a fibrotic condition.

## Relevance

This Special Issue collects cutting-edge molecular research on endometriosis, covering:
- Inflammatory and fibrotic pathways (IL-6/miR-21/TGF-β, IL-17)
- Biomarkers for sexual dysfunction and pain (IL-8, BDNF)
- Molecular distinctions between eutopic and ectopic endometrium
- Adenomyosis co-morbidity mechanisms
