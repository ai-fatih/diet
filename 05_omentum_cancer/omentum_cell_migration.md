# Cell Migration in Endometriosis Responds to Omentum-Derived Molecular Cues Similar to Ovarian Cancer

**Published:** IJMS, February 2025  
**DOI:** 10.3390/ijms26051822

- Human omentum (apron of adipose tissue in abdominopelvic cavity) provides molecular cues for trans-coelomic spread.
- Omental conditioned media promotes cell migration in BOTH endometriosis and ovarian cancer primary cultures.
- Omental CM contains high levels of: HGF, SDF-1α, MCP-1, VEGF-A, IL-6, IL-8.
- HGF/c-MET signaling mediates cell migration in both conditions.
- c-MET inhibition (PHA665752) significantly reduces migration — potential therapeutic target.
- PTTG1 consistently upregulated in migrated cells of BOTH endometriosis and ovarian cancer.
- Omentectomy during endometriosis surgery is proposed as a clinical trial concept.
- Endometriosis is benign but shares distribution pattern with ovarian cancer metastasis.
- Endometriosis associated with 2-3x increased risk of ovarian cancer.
- Oncogenic mutations (ARID1A, PIK3CA, KRAS, PPP2R1A) found in endometriotic tissue.

## Краткая справка (русский)

Сальник (omentum) выделяет молекулярные сигналы (HGF, SDF-1α, MCP-1, VEGF-A, IL-6, IL-8), стимулирующие миграцию клеток как при эндометриозе, так и при раке яичников. Сигналинг HGF/c-MET — ключевой; ингибитор PHA665752 снижает миграцию. PTTG1 повышен в мигрировавших клетках обоих заболеваний. Предложена концепция клинического исследования оментэктомии при хирургии эндометриоза. Эндометриоз повышает риск рака яичников в 2–3 раза; в очагах найдены онкогенные мутации (ARID1A, PIK3CA, KRAS, PPP2R1A).
